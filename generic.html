Federico Citterich is a Rome-based science journalist and communicator who focuses on climate change, sustainability, and biodiversity. He currently works as a TV journalist at Canale 21 and as a science communicator at FLIM LABS. He also collaborates with the Institute of Polar Sciences of the Italian National Research Council, with which he took part in a research expedition to the North Pole in September 2024. In addition, he contributes as a freelance science journalist to various Italian and international outlets, including L'Osservatore Romano, Vatican News, Onyx, and Newsweek.

Pancreatic Cancer: The Three-Part Series in Summary

Pancreatic cancer remains one of the most lethal malignancies, with a five-year survival rate lingering below 10%. Recognizing this critical gap, the LaserBlood project emerges as a beacon of hope, aiming to revolutionize pancreatic cancer diagnosis through innovative biophotonic technologies.
